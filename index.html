Evan Angelone

Mathematics PhD Student · Northeastern University

I am a PhD student in the Department of Mathematics at Northeastern University, advised by Egon Schulte. My primary research area is in discrete geometry, with a focus on abstract polytopes and the classification of certain chiral automorphism groups, as well as the combinatorics of families of convex polytopes. Previously, I received an AS in Mathematics from Raritan Valley Community College in 2018 as a first-generation college student. After graduating from RVCC, I went on to earn a BA in Mathematics from Reed College in 2021 where I was advised by Jamie Pommersheim, under whom I wrote my year-long senior thesis.

Dissertation Materials

Which groups arise from chiral polytopes? The simplicity of the question's phrasing masks the true scale of the problem at hand: even among groups of small order, say, at most 2000, there are 50 billion possible candidates to sift through… over 49 billion of those factorize as \(2^n\), and another 400 million factorize as \(2^n p\) for some odd prime p.
In this dissertation, we develop the theory of abstract chiral 3-polytopes of order \(2^n p\) for odd primes \(p\).
