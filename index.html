Evan Angelone

Mathematics PhD Student · Northeastern University

I am a doctoral candidate in the Department of Mathematics at Northeastern University, advised by Egon Schulte. My research focuses on discrete geometry, particularly abstract polytopes, chiral symmetry, and the combinatorics of convex polytopes. As an educator, I am deeply invested in reimagining the typical pedestrian classroom experience as a space where mathematics feels inviting, rigorous, and explorative. Previously, I earned an AS from Raritan Valley Community College (2018) as a first-generation college student and a BA from Reed College (2021), where I wrote my year-long senior thesis under Jamie Pommersheim.
